## Live Data

*This experiment explored how live data can be accessed and translated into dynamic visual systems. I used real-time weather data from the Open-Meteo platform as an external data source and visualised it through a p5.js sketch. Building on my previous experiments, I incorporated kaleidoscope-inspired patterns to create a cohesive visual language that connects environmental conditions with emotional expression.*

## Process 

*Weather Data Collection*

*To investigate live data, I sourced weather information for Auckland using the Open-Meteo platform. I focused on variables such as cloud cover, wind speed, and weather conditions, as these values change continuously and provide insight into environmental patterns. I documented this data through weather charts, which helped me understand temporal variations and informed my visual mapping decisions.*

*Translation into p5.js*

*I translated selected weather variables into a dynamic visual system using the p5.js web editor. Each data point influenced specific visual properties:*

*Cloud Cover → Controlled the level of symmetry in the kaleidoscope patterns.*
*Wind Speed → Influenced the size and thickness of the shapes.*
*Weather Code/Conditions → Affected colour and visual intensity.*

*These mappings allowed the visualisation to respond to changing environmental conditions, creating an evolving and expressive representation of live data.*

*Connection to Previous Experiments*

*This experiment intentionally connects to my earlier work. In Week 1, I explored emotional data through a hand-drawn portrait, and in Week 2, I developed interactive kaleidoscope patterns. By using similar visual language in this experiment, I created continuity across the projects.*

*The kaleidoscope form was particularly effective in illustrating how both weather and emotions share rhythmic and shifting qualities. Just as weather patterns fluctuate over time, emotional states also change in response to external and internal influences. This conceptual link allowed me to bridge objective environmental data with subjective human experience.*

## Refelection 

*This experiment demonstrated how live data can be transformed into expressive visual systems rather than simply presented as numerical information. Working with weather data allowed me to observe patterns and rhythms that evolve over time, reinforcing the dynamic nature of live data.*

*One of the most valuable insights was the conceptual relationship between weather and mood. Weather conditions such as brightness, cloudiness, or windiness often influence emotional states, making the data feel relatable and human. By representing these conditions through kaleidoscope patterns, I was able to create a visual metaphor for this connection.*

*A challenge I encountered was determining how to meaningfully map numerical data to visual properties. Through iterative experimentation, I refined these relationships to ensure that the visual output clearly reflected the underlying data while maintaining an expressive aesthetic.*

*If I had more time, I would further develop this project by integrating real-time personal mood data alongside weather information, allowing both internal and external conditions to influence a single visual system.*
